Other schools mosaic
Mosaic mural at Yuanlin Senior High School


Yuanlin High School's Zhengxin Arts and Culture Plaza features two enormous (5m x 6m) exquisitely inlaid mosaic murals. The east mural is a reproduction of Zhang Huancai's "Hometown of White Clouds," depicting the Bagua Mountains to the east of Yuanlin, symbolizing the school motto "Simplicity." The west mural is "Striving with the Sea," painted by Professor Shi Bingxi of the Department of Fine Arts at National Taiwan Normal University, symbolizing the school motto "Diligence." Both murals were completed over six months by Professor Li Yixun and the Hongmaogang Mosaic Art Team. Each mural requires an additional 1-2 months of meticulous installation on-site to complete the final piece.
Mosaic wall around the gate of Dongshan Elementary School in Yuanlin City, Changhua County


This mosaic mural is part of the Tourism Administration's initiative to promote aesthetics in scenic areas. It combines the Tourism Administration's mascot with the Dongshan Elementary School's mascot on an exterior wall, creating a joyful and vibrant atmosphere.
Mosaic of Cundong Elementary School, Dacun Township, Changhua County

Behind the flagpole in the school's central courtyard, there is a mosaic tile pattern featuring two butterflies fluttering about. The colors are rich and the shapes are beautiful; it's a beautiful piece of public art worth stopping to admire.
Mosaic of Fuxing Elementary School, Ershui Township, Changhua County

The school gate is decorated with mosaic tiles, making it hard to pass by without being drawn to the vibrant colors.

On the facade of the school's student activity center building, there are two huge mosaic murals. One depicts symbols including hands, brains, eyes, and various natural and astronomical images, symbolizing the expectation of diligent study and skillful use of the mind. The other shows a small boat sailing on a blue sea with turbulent waves, symbolizing blessings for students to brave the winds and soar to the heavens.

The walls of the corridors also feature mosaic tile murals.

Public building mosaic
Large-scale 3D mosaic in the outdoor space of Yuanlin Performing Arts Hall.The three works, created in 2002 by Mr. Hung Hung-Yi using mixed media including concrete, stainless steel, ceramic slabs, porcelain bottles, colored tiles, and colored glass beads, are titled "Life is a Dream." Composed of three pieces—"Ballet Dancer," "Universal Musician," and "Life Actor"—the works reflect the Yuanlin Performing Arts Hall's function and purpose of "Gathering of All People, Connecting All Under One Roof." They represent dance performances, classical music, and traditional opera, employing innocent and slightly childlike forms and humorous expressions and movements to symbolize the subtle influence and emotional impact of performing arts such as music, drama, and dance on people's lives. Through brightly colored and vibrant artworks, the works express the joy that performing arts bring to people's lives, hoping that the public will have a completely new experience when entering the performing arts hall.



The mosaic installation art at Yuanlin Performing Arts Hall is mainly part of the "Dreamlike Life" series, consisting of three works: "Ballet Dancer," "Universal Musician," and "Life Actor." Using materials such as concrete, ceramic slabs, porcelain bottles, colored tiles, and glass beads, the installation presents a playful and vibrant image of dance, music, and traditional opera integrated into a gathering of the masses.

The mosaic mural at the entrance of the performance hall is a colorful stone mural titled "Come Have Tea,"(lái chī chá來喫茶) created by artist Chen Jingrong(陳景容).
