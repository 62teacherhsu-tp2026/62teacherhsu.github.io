Mosaic murals at TaiPing
Mosaic murals on the wall Mosaic murals on building walls

Exploring the beauty of TaiPing Elementary School begins with its school wall. The north wall is decorated with 12 different types of ceramic tile mosaic murals, each with a different meaning. Let's appreciate them together!
Mosaic murals on the wall

Our school's mosaic murals are not only the most numerous in the county, but also of very high quality, possessing both aesthetic and educational value. Each mosaic brick on the north wall is an exquisite mural, covering a wide range of subjects and rich in metaphorical meaning, making them truly dazzling.

Two koalas represent twin brothers.

Two fish represent loving siblings.

Two deer represent deep brotherly love and mutual respect.

Two frogs represent fulfilling relationships.

Two pheasants represent radiant beauty.

Two sheep represent kindness and cuteness.

Two cranes represent soaring through the boundless sky.
Two rats represent warmth and peace.
Two rabbits represent universal love.
Two dogs represent unwavering loyalty.

Two dolphins represent endless joy.
Two butterflies represent being a perfect pair.

Mosaic murals on building walls
Both sides of the school building are two large ceramic tile mosaic murals titled "Landing on the Moon" and "Soaring Ambitions," while the front wall of the school building features a ceramic tile mosaic mural of sunflowers.

The mural depicting the landing on the moon symbolizes the aspirations of Taiping Elementary School students for a bright future.



The spiral staircase next to the lunar landing mural echoes the mural's design, resembling a space shuttle taking off.


The idiom "天馬行空" (tiān mǎ xíng kōng) originates from a description in the Book of Han of the Heavenly Emperor's divine horse galloping across the sky. Later, it came to describe someone with a rich imagination and outstanding literary talent. This painting symbolizes the hope that the students of Tai Ping Elementary School will all possess exceptional talents and be able to fully develop their abilities.


Sunflower mosaic on the school building wall

The facades on both sides of the school gate are also decorated with sunflower tile mosaic.
